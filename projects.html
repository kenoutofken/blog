ReadAloud: A Children's Learning App (UX and UI Design Concept)

Project Rationale

The "ReadAloud" app concept was born from my personal experience working in a restaurant as a server. I often saw parents trying to keep their children occupied with games and cartoons on their devices. I realized this was a lost opportunity to engage kids in a more educational experience during their screen time. My concept for the "ReadAloud" app is to use AI to create a karaoke-like experience for kids, that encourages them to read the ebooks out loud, with the AI acting as a tool to help kids with words that they struggle with. The app tracks progress to inform parents of their child's learning, but ultimately the goal is to attract children with an experience that is fun and engaging. AI will also be used to create animations for each page to immerse children in their reading.

Defining the Problem

Parents often struggle to find activities for their children that allows them to have fun while also facilitating growth and learning. Cartoons and games are often what they turn to from my own personal observations, but these activities are passive, and do not provide any tools for the child to learn nor any ways for parents to document their child's educational development. “ReadAloud” aims to solve this problem by creating an app that is easy and fun to use for kids, while also creating a learning experience that fosters growth. It combines the educational experience of reading with animated cartoons. All the while, the app provides tools that allow parents to see their child's learning through achievements and progress tracking.

Identifying the User

The app is designed for young children between the ages of 6-11. Children's books are provided at various levels to allow for children across the age range and skill level to benefit from their experience.
